I am in my third year as a student in college. I have thus far been able to use my major in Liberal Studies to build the skills and qualities I need to learn, think and create on my own. Currently, school is my full time job as I do spend 40+ hours on school work everyday. I take pride in my work and being a student. Ideally, I have found myself building my habits around what would be considered a full time job. I am a student at the University of California Riverside and uphold the values of honesty, academic integrity, respect, diversity and giving attention to quality and excellence in completing assignments both in my educational life and personal. Previously, I was a Mentor through a program named Highlander Early Start (HESA), at the University of California Riverside. I assisted in finding campus resources or staff assistance and provided encouragement and motivation to new students. This is one of the many things I take pride in because as a first generation student, I was in a place where I felt I didn't belong. Many of the students I mentored gained skills prior to starting college that would help them their next four years. I didn't have anyone telling me what to do nor did I know how to navigate that new chapter in my life. I love helping others because it makes me happy that I was able to impact one's life even if it was a little. This connects to a reason why I chose to go to college and why I chose to pursue a degree with a major in Liberal Studies. I aspire to become an elementary school teacher and will be the next future teacher. A Bachelor's Degree with a major in Liberal Studies is perfect for me compared to a major in Education because Liberal Studies is broad and valuable in giving me the skills of critical thinking, adaptability, problem solving, etc. I want to be a teacher who could apply what she learned to her classroom. Teaching seven to eight year old children means that I will have a great impact on how they grow and mature as students. It is their most impressionable years and children learn what they see. I want to be a great example to the future and motivate students. I will act as a role model, change lives for the better and influence and shape the next generation.

Competencies: Communication, Teamwork and Resilience.
